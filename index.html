DHANANJAY  KUMAR

Dhananjay Kumar is a PhD student in the Department of Mechanical Engineering at Indian Institute of Technology Guwahati, India. He received his Master of Technology (M.Tech) degree in Thermal Power Engineering from National Institute of Technology Tiruchirappalli Tamil Nadu, India in July 2019. He received his Bachelor of Technology (B.Tech) in Mechanical Engineering, from R.P Sharma Institute of Technology, Patna in August 2015. He joined IIT Guwahati in July 2020 under the supervision of Dr. Pranab Kumar Mondal. He is a recipient of the prestigious Prime Minister's Research Fellowship (PMRF), awarded to the country's excellent doctoral candidates to pursue PhD in the areas of Mechanical engineering, IIT Guwahati. He is currently working on mixing in micro-channel with inlet swirl decay for Newtonian and non-Newtonian fluid using the Finite Volume Method (FVM).

Research Objective

  • Efficient mixing in microscale should address the biological response time, reaction rates of the chemicals or biological enzymes, and gradients of partial pressure of essential microlevel species in vivo systems etc.
  • Continuing with the previous point, no significant attempt has been made to investigate micro-mixing by varying the geometrical shape of the fluidic channel (impact of swirl at the inlet) and using external fields such as magnetic fields.

    • Objective 1

    • To investigate the mixing of constituent components (Newtonian fluid) in a cylindrical narrow fluidic channel in presence of an inlet swirl.

    Our objective is to get the uniform concentration i.e. mixing is achieved within a short length by considering the swirl (secondary flow) and obtained the efficient mixing index quantitatively.

    Objective 2

    • To investigate Droplet-based mixing in a microfluidic channel under magnetic field variation: an experimental and numerical study.

    Our objective is to obtained the droplet based efficient mixing in a crossflow T-junction with Mineral oil as continuous phase and DI Water based ferrofluid as dispersed phase under the variation of magnetic field.
